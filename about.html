Murda Hill: A Little Spooky, A Lot Charming

Murda Hill was born under an Atlanta sky that couldn't decide between rain or moonlight, which, honestly, explains a lot. From an early age, she had a knack for turning the ordinary into something just a little more magical—turning childhood bedtime stories into full-blown theatrical productions and convincing friends that the best kind of fun usually came with a flashlight and a few exaggerated ghost stories.

Filmmaker, writer, and lover of all things a little eerie but never too scary, Murda has spent her life creating stories that make people smile just as much as they send a shiver up their spine. She believes that the best tales live somewhere between laughter and goosebumps, and she’s made it her mission to bring them to life.

Notable Achievements

  • Winner of the Moonlit Reel Award for her haunting short film The Last Whisper (rumored to be cursed—watch at your own risk).
  • Creator of the Spooky Sagas Podcast, where listeners experience tales so chilling they’ve been known to cause spontaneous candle flickering.
  • Host of the Gothic Gala, an annual event where filmmakers, artists, and well-dressed specters gather to celebrate horror in all its glory.
  • Successfully made a ghost laugh, which is both an artistic and scientific achievement.

The Curious Case of Fuzzywink Gobblefunk

Then there’s Fuzzywink Gobblefunk—a peculiar, wisecracking gnome wizard she met on a particularly foggy night while scouting locations for a film. He had a sharp tongue, a smirk that suggested he knew too much, and a way of appearing just when Murda least expected (or most needed) a distraction.

Despite the occasional distraction from mischievous gnomes, Murda continues to create stories that make people laugh, wonder, and maybe—just maybe—check under their beds once before going to sleep.
